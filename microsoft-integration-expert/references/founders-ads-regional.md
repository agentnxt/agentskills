### Microsoft Co-Founder Story
- **1968** — Wrote first software program (tic-tac-toe) at age 13 on Lakeside School's GE computer
- **1972** — Co-founded Traf-O-Data with Paul Allen — sold traffic-counting systems to local governments
- **1973** — Entered Harvard University to study mathematics and computer science
- **1975** — **Co-founded Microsoft** with childhood friend **Paul Allen** in Albuquerque, New Mexico
  - Name "Micro-soft" coined by Allen (microcomputer + software), later shortened to Microsoft
  - First product: BASIC interpreter for the Altair 8800 (MITS)
  - Microsoft became independent of MITS in 1976
- **1980** — Landmark IBM deal: Microsoft licensed MS-DOS to IBM for the IBM PC
- **1986** — **Microsoft IPO** (March 13, 1986) — company valued at $520M, Gates owned 45% (~$234M)
- **1987** — Became **world's youngest billionaire** at age 31 (post-IPO stock appreciation)
- **1995** — Became **world's richest person** for the first time (net worth: $12.9 billion)
- **1999** — Became **first centibillionaire** — net worth briefly exceeded $100 billion
- **2000** — Stepped down as CEO; remained as Chairman and Chief Software Architect
- **2000** — Co-founded **Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation** with wife Melinda French Gates
- **2008** — Last full day at Microsoft (June 2008, age 52); transitioned to full-time philanthropy
- **2014** — Stepped down as Chairman; became Technology Adviser to incoming CEO **Satya Nadella**
- **2020** — **Resigned from Microsoft's board** entirely (March 2020)
- **2021** — Divorced Melinda French Gates after 27 years of marriage
- **2025** — Still does occasional product reviews for Microsoft (confirmed January 2025)

Note: Net worth declining intentionally — Gates pledged in May 2025 to donate 99% of remaining fortune (~$107B) to the Gates Foundation, which plans to spend $200 billion over 20 years and close by 2045.

### Cascade Investment LLC
Gates's private wealth management vehicle. Key holdings:
- **Republic Services** — waste disposal
- **Deere & Co.** — agricultural machinery / tractors
- **Ecolab** — water/food safety
- **Canadian National Railway**
- Large US farmland portfolio (Gates is the largest private farmland owner in the US)
- Real estate including "Xanadu 2.0" — 66,000 sq ft home in Medina, WA overlooking Lake Washington

### Philanthropy
**Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation** (now Gates Foundation post-divorce):
- Co-founded 2000 with Melinda French Gates
- One of the world's largest private philanthropic organizations
- Focus: Global health, poverty alleviation, education, vaccine distribution
- Gates has donated ~$100 billion to charitable causes (2025)
- Gates + Warren Buffett co-launched **The Giving Pledge** (2010) — billionaires commit majority of wealth to charity
- Foundation plans to close by **December 2045**
- Melinda stepped down June 2024 with a $12.5B commitment for her own philanthropic work (Pivotal Ventures)
- Foundation has ~41% funding from Warren Buffett (ongoing gift since 2006)

### Paul Allen — Co-Founder
- **Born:** January 21, 1953, Seattle, WA
- **Died:** October 15, 2018 (aged 65, non-Hodgkin's lymphoma)
- Met Gates at Lakeside School; introduced Gates to the Altair 8800 opportunity
- Left Microsoft in 1983 due to Hodgkin's disease
- Founded Vulcan Inc. (private investment), owned Seattle Seahawks and Portland Trail Blazers
- Philanthropist: donated $2+ billion to STEM, brain science, arts

## 📢 MICROSOFT ADVERTISING (ads.microsoft.com)

### Platform Overview
**Microsoft Advertising** (formerly Bing Ads) is Microsoft's end-to-end digital advertising platform.

URL: **ads.microsoft.com**
Formerly: Bing Ads → rebranded to Microsoft Advertising

### Scale & Reach
| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Monthly users reached | **1.4 billion** across Bing, Edge, Outlook, LinkedIn, Xbox |
| Monthly Bing searches | **1+ billion** |
| US search market share | ~7.56% overall, **17.55% desktop** |
| Global desktop search share | **12.21%** |
| Unique US users (not on Google) | **66 million** |
| Microsoft Advertising revenue (2025) | **$13.88 billion** (FY2025) |
| Revenue growth (2024→2025) | +13.4% |
| Projected revenue 2026 | $19.53 billion |

### Ad Placements
Ads appear across:
- **Bing** — primary search engine
- **Yahoo** and **DuckDuckGo** — partner search networks
- **MSN** — Microsoft's news/content portal
- **Microsoft Outlook** (Outlook.com) — email native ads
- **Microsoft Edge** — browser native placements
- **Xbox** — gaming ads (App campaigns: King/Candy Crush, Microsoft Casual Games)
- **Windows Start Menu** and **Microsoft Store** — reaching 1 billion+ Windows users
- **LinkedIn** — via professional targeting data
- **Netflix** — partnership for expanded audience reach

### Ad Formats
- **Search Ads** — text-based PPC on Bing + partner engines
- **Shopping Ads** — product ads via Microsoft Merchant Center
- **Dynamic Search Ads** — auto-generated from site content
- **Audience Campaigns (MSAN)** — display/native on MSN, Outlook, Edge, Microsoft 365
- **Performance Max (PMax)** — AI-automated cross-channel (pilot 2025, LinkedIn integration)
- **Copilot Ads** — ads within Bing Generative Search (live in English, French, German; Spanish/Japanese launching)
- **Brand Agents** — AI-powered two-way brand conversations (new 2025)
- **Audio Ads** — AI-generated audio in Copilot Daily on MSN
- **Gaming Ads** — Xbox console, PC, mobile (King, Microsoft Casual Games)

### Key Differentiators vs Google Ads

| Feature | Microsoft Advertising | Google Ads |
|---|---|---|
| Average CPC | ~$1.54 | ~$1–$2+ (higher competition) |
| LinkedIn targeting | ✅ Exclusive — job title, company, industry, company size | ❌ Not available |
| Competition level | 1/2 to 1/3 fewer advertisers | Very high competition |
| Desktop search share | 17.55% US desktop | ~80% US desktop |
| B2B audience | 80% are business decision-makers | Mixed |
| Audience demographics | 70% aged 35–65; 33% earn $100K+ | Broader age range |
| Google Ads import | ✅ One-click import | — |
| AI creative tool | Copilot in Ads Studio | Google AI |

### Targeting Capabilities
- **LinkedIn Profile Targeting** — job title, company name, industry, company size (unique to Microsoft Ads)
- **In-market audiences** — intent-based targeting from Bing search behavior
- **Remarketing & impression-based remarketing**
- **Customer match** — upload CRM lists
- **Similar audiences**
- **First-party signals**: Bing search, Edge browsing, Office 365 usage patterns

### Microsoft Ads AI Features (2025)
- **Copilot in Ads** — AI ad creation assistant within Microsoft Advertising platform
- **Ads Studio** — centralized creative management with AI asset generation
- **Performance Max (PMax)** — automated multi-channel delivery with LinkedIn integration
- **Brand Agents** — AI-powered consumer conversations for brands
- **Universal Event Tracking (UET)** — cross-channel conversion tracking
- **Microsoft Clarity** integration — post-click behavior analytics

### Advertising API
For developers and agencies:
- Bing Ads API / Microsoft Advertising API → learn.microsoft.com/en-us/advertising/
- Supports: Bulk Service, Campaign Management Service
- Ad types: Search, Shopping, Audience, Dynamic
- SDKs available for .NET, Java, Python, PHP

### Portal & Entry Point
