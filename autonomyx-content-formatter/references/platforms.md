## Blog / Article

- **Length**: 600–2,500 words (short-form to long-form)
- **Format**: H1 title, H2/H3 subheadings, paragraphs of 2–4 sentences, bullet lists OK
- **Tone**: Varies — informational, thought leadership, conversational, SEO-optimised
- **Hook**: Strong opening sentence or question; establish the "why read this" in first 2 lines
- **Structure**: Hook → Problem/Context → Body (3–7 sections) → CTA/Conclusion
- **CTA**: Subscribe, share, read more, contact
- **SEO**: Use keyword naturally in title, first paragraph, and 2–3 subheadings
- **Images**: Recommend placement but don't describe them unless asked
- **Hashtags**: None

## Email Newsletter

- **Subject line**: 40–60 chars; curiosity, benefit, or urgency driven; avoid spam words (FREE, URGENT)
- **Preview text**: 80–100 chars; extends subject line, not a repeat
- **Length**: 150–500 words for regular; up to 1,000 for deep-dive
- **Format**: Short paragraphs (1–3 sentences), scannable, single-column mental model
- **Tone**: Personal, conversational, like writing to one person
- **Hook**: First sentence must be punchy — the reader decides in 2 seconds
- **CTA**: One primary CTA; button-style label (e.g. "Read the full piece →")
- **Personalisation**: Use "you" frequently; avoid "our readers" or third-person
- **Hashtags**: None
- **Output format**: Include Subject Line + Preview Text + Body

## Facebook

- **Length**: 40–80 words optimal for engagement; up to 400 words for storytelling posts
- **Format**: Short paragraphs, line breaks for readability, emojis optional
- **Tone**: Warm, community-oriented, conversational, inclusive
- **Hook**: First 2 lines must hook before "See More" truncation
- **CTA**: Question to drive comments, or link with context
- **Hashtags**: 1–3 max; hashtags less important here than other platforms
- **Media**: Always suggest pairing with image/video
- **Algorithm tip**: Posts that spark comments > reactions > shares; end with a question

## Instagram

- **Caption length**: 125–150 chars for feed (truncated at ~125 before "more"); up to 2,200 max
- **Format**: Hook in first line, then expand, line breaks using spacing trick, emojis to add visual rhythm
- **Tone**: Aspirational, visual, personal, brand-voice dependent
- **Hook**: Must work in first 125 chars — the rest is "more"
- **Hashtags**: 3–5 targeted hashtags (post body or first comment); avoid hashtag stuffing
- **CTA**: "Link in bio", "Save this", "Tag someone who…", "Drop your answer below"
- **Stories vs Feed**: Feed = curated; Stories = raw, ephemeral, conversational
- **Reels caption**: Even shorter — 1–2 punchy lines max
- **Output format**: Specify if feed post, reel, or story

## LinkedIn

- **Length**: 150–300 words for high engagement; up to 700 for thought leadership
- **Format**: One sentence per line (LinkedIn renders line breaks); avoid dense paragraphs; bold sparingly using asterisks
- **Tone**: Professional but human; first-person storytelling performs best; avoid corporate-speak
- **Hook**: First 2 lines shown before "...see more" — must compel the click; use a bold claim, question, or counterintuitive statement
- **Structure**: Hook → Story or insight → Lesson/takeaway → CTA
- **CTA**: Ask a question, invite comments, or soft-link to article
- **Hashtags**: 3–5 relevant hashtags at the end
- **Algorithm tip**: Dwell time matters — write content people pause and read fully; early comments boost reach significantly
- **Avoid**: Clickbait, excessive self-promotion, "I'm humbled to announce" clichés

## Medium

- **Length**: 1,000–2,500 words ideal; supports long-form journalism style
- **Format**: Similar to blog — H2 subheadings, pull quotes, clean prose
- **Tone**: Thoughtful, essayistic, intellectual, personal narrative or deep analysis
- **Hook**: Establish the central tension or surprising idea in the first paragraph
- **Structure**: Essay or narrative arc; avoid listicles unless they serve the piece
- **CTA**: Claps, follow, comment, or link to newsletter
- **Hashtags**: None (uses "tags" — 5 topic tags max)
- **Distribution**: Aim for Medium Partner Program publications for amplification
- **Unique note**: Medium readers expect depth; don't water down; reward them for reading

## Pinterest

- **Pin description**: 100–500 chars; keyword-rich for search discoverability
- **Title**: 100 chars max; descriptive and searchable
- **Tone**: Inspirational, how-to, instructional, aspirational
- **Format**: Bullet points or step-by-step work well; highly visual platform so describe what the image/graphic would show
- **Hook**: Lead with the benefit or the end result ("How to…", "X ways to…", "The ultimate guide to…")
- **CTA**: "Save this pin", "Click to read more", "Try this today"
- **Hashtags**: 2–5 relevant hashtags
- **SEO**: Pinterest is a search engine — use natural keyword phrases people would search for
- **Output format**: Include Pin Title + Description + suggested image concept

## Podcast Script

- **Length**: Segment length varies — intro (30–60s), main segment (3–15 min), outro (30–60s)
- **Format**: Written for the ear, not the eye — conversational, spoken-word rhythm
- **Tone**: Natural, flowing, like talking to a friend or colleague
- **Structure**: Hook/teaser → Introduction → Main content (with natural transitions) → Summary → CTA/Outro
- **Language**: Short sentences; avoid complex clauses; use contractions (it's, we're, you'll); read aloud test
- **Transitions**: Use verbal signposts ("Now, here's where it gets interesting…", "So what does that mean for you?")
- **CTA**: Subscribe, leave a review, visit link, follow on social
- **Output format**: Include [INTRO], [MAIN], [OUTRO] sections with approximate timing

## Snapchat

- **Caption length**: Very short — 0–50 characters; often no caption at all
- **Format**: Punchy one-liners, emoji-heavy, visual-first
- **Tone**: Playful, raw, unfiltered, youthful, FOMO-driven
- **Hook**: Instant — if it doesn't hit in 1 second, it's gone
- **Audience**: Predominantly Gen Z; informal, trend-aware language
- **CTA**: Swipe up, reply, or tap for more (Stories context)
- **Hashtags**: Rarely used; not a hashtag platform
- **Output format**: Suggest caption + overlay text concept for the visual

## Substack

- **Length**: 500–2,000 words; readers opt in so they expect depth
- **Format**: Personal essay, newsletter hybrid; can use headers but prose-first
- **Tone**: Intimate, authoritative, opinionated; the writer's voice is the product
- **Hook**: First paragraph must reward the reader immediately — why does this matter today?
- **Structure**: Strong thesis → exploration → payoff; don't bury the lead
- **CTA**: Subscribe (free or paid), share, reply with thoughts
- **Hashtags**: None
- **Unique note**: Substack readers are paying attention — write like it; no filler, no padding; every paragraph earns its place
- **Subject line**: Treat like email — 40–60 chars; curiosity + value

## Threads

- **Length**: Up to 500 characters per post; works as single post or thread
- **Format**: Conversational, opinion-led, short bursts; thread format for longer takes
- **Tone**: Casual, witty, direct, community-feel; less polished than LinkedIn
- **Hook**: Lead with the hottest take or most interesting point
- **Hashtags**: Minimal — 0–2; Threads is early-stage on hashtag use
- **CTA**: "Thoughts?", "Agree or disagree?", "Add yours below"
- **Algorithm tip**: Replies and reshares drive reach; write content that provokes a response
- **Output format**: If thread, number each post (1/, 2/, etc.)

## Twitter / X

- **Length**: 280 chars per tweet; threads up to 25 tweets
- **Format**: Single tweet or thread; short punchy sentences; line breaks for rhythm
- **Tone**: Direct, opinionated, witty, concise; personality-driven
- **Hook**: Tweet 1 must standalone AND make people want to read the thread
- **Thread structure**: Hook tweet → numbered points or story beats → summary/CTA tweet
- **Hashtags**: 1–2 max; hashtags reduce engagement if overused
- **CTA**: "RT if you agree", "What's your take?", "Thread 🧵", link at end of thread
- **Algorithm tip**: Replies in first 30 min boost reach; avoid links in first tweet of a thread
- **Output format**: For threads, show each tweet numbered with character count

## WhatsApp / Telegram

- **Length**: 50–300 words; very short for broadcast messages; longer OK for deep communities/channels
- **Format**: Conversational prose or bullet points; bold using *asterisks*; emojis for warmth
- **Tone**: Personal, warm, direct — like a message from a trusted friend or community manager
- **Hook**: First line must be clear about what this is and why it matters
- **CTA**: Reply, tap link, share with someone, "Let me know if you have questions"
- **Hashtags**: None
- **Links**: Include clearly; don't bury them
- **Unique note**: WhatsApp is high-trust, low-noise — don't over-broadcast; every message should feel worth their time
- **Output format**: Specify if broadcast message, group message, or channel post

## YouTube Script

- **Length**: Varies by format — Short (60s), Standard (7–15 min), Long-form (15–30+ min)
- **Format**: Written for spoken delivery; hook, content, CTA; include [B-ROLL], [PAUSE], [CUT TO] cues if needed
- **Tone**: Energetic, clear, direct; match to channel persona
- **Hook**: First 30 seconds are critical — state the promise, tease the payoff, don't waste time
- **Structure**: Hook (0–30s) → Intro/context (30–60s) → Main content → Recap → CTA
- **CTA**: Subscribe, like, comment, watch next video, link in description
- **Pacing**: Write for a natural speaking pace (~130 words/minute); break up dense info with "Now let's talk about…" transitions
- **Output format**: Include [SECTION LABELS] and approximate timestamps for longer scripts
